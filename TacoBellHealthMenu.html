Taco Bell Health Menu

By Briana Chahine

 

Good morning eagles! That’s right, Taco Bell is one of the top healthiest fast food chains. An article on Eatthis.com rated Taco Bell to a B, which is pretty good compared to the grades that other fast food chains got such as a C, a D, and an F.
According to USA Today, “The fast-food chain has made a series of changes over the years, from eliminating artificial flavors and trans fat to the low-calorie ‘Fresco’ menu and reducing sodium across the menu by 15% since 2008. The chain now plans to eliminate XL sodas, cut an additional 10% of sodium across the menu, and remove some antibiotics from its chicken by early 2017.”
Another source from PJ Media also stated and I quote: "Whether you're looking for vegetarian, made without gluten, high protein, lower calorie/lower fat, in the mood for something indulgent, or all of the above, Taco Bell has food for all."
An analysis published in JAMA Internal Medicine on Monday found that 75 percent of men and 67 percent of women ages 25 and older are now overweight or obese. Obesity has appeared to have high statistics in the United States. When there is high counts of fat, calorie, and sodium contents in foods it can lead to many health issues, such as obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular conditions. That’s why it is very helpful to always read the nutrition facts when ordering food at a fast food restaurant. That’s where the nutrition calculator comes in. The fact that Taco Bell has a new healthier menu, shows that Taco Bell wants to help reduce the percentage of obesity.
