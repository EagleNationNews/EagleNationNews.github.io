Android Special Feature

 
By John Coker

Nearly One million Android phones were infected by hackers recently. “That discovery comes from computer researchers at Check Point, a cybersecurity firm”. On Wednesday, Google said “The attack was from a malware that infects your phone through certain apps from third party confirmed to CNNMoney the nature and extent of the problem”. According to Google, The hackers received digital “Tokens” to access these accounts and phones, Google also claim’s the hackers have not yet tapped the information and passwords.Infected Android smartphones begin to install other, legitimate Android apps -- then rate them highly. This fraudulently inflates their reputation, according to CheckPoint researchers. Make sure to only download applications from the official Google appstore and stay clear from third party developers! First party developers is the group Google trusts as an actual business. Third party developers are people like me and you. Basically First party developers are professional and Third party developers are beginners and not licensed. These Developers are new to developing and are trying to get their work out there. Some of these Developers might have viruses or very dangerous malware that can affect your device if you install them. So, Be careful with what things you download!
