FaceBook Targeting Teens

 
by Khanh Nguyen

Facebook is exploiting vulnerable teenagers into buying advertiser’s products that could potentially “help” them. According to Forbes, “ The document specifies what emotional states Facebook is interested in helping advertisers pinpoint; ‘stressed, ‘a failure’, ‘worthless,’ ‘insecure,’ ‘defeated,’ ‘anxious,’ ‘silly,’ ‘useless,’ ‘stupid,’ and ‘overwhelmed’.” This means that Facebook can use this as an advantage to manipulate teen’s feeds for their youth marketing to increase their brands. Let’s say for example that a teen is worried about their acne, and have been harass at school by others kid because of his condition. This lower their self-esteem so they decided to post online in Facebooks of how they feel. Facebook can use this to track their post based on a system, so they advertise a “instant cure for acne in a day!.” So, the teen who read this advertisement might feel curious of wanting to buy that product. “Per one report, 49% of under 13 year-olds use Facebook.” Forbes said. This percentage is very alarming because many teens have not mature their logic of thinking, so when an interesting advertisement pop up that say they can fix the teen problem by using this product.
